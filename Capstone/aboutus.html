ABOUT WCS

The Wildlife Conservation Society (WCS), formerly known as the New York Zoological Society, is a non-profit organization founded in 1895. With a mission to conserve wildlife and wild places around the world, WCS has a rich history of achievement. They've played a critical role in protecting over 350 million acres of habitat and the species that call it home. WCS works across over 65 countries and focuses on 14 priority regions that represent nearly half of the world's biodiversity. Their unique approach combines scientific research, field conservation efforts, and education programs at world-class zoos and aquariums like the Bronx Zoo and Central Park Zoo. Get involved with WCS today and help ensure a future for wildlife and wild places around the world.

We oversee five wildlife parks/aquariums in New York City including: The Bronx Zoo, Central Park Zoo, New York Aquarium, Prospect Park Zoo, and The Queens Zoo.

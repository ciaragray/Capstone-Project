About WCS

The Wildlife Conservation Society (Formerly known as The New York Zoological Society) is a 501(c)(3) Non-Profit Organization founded in 1895 by the state of New York. Our goal is to preserve the wildlife that is native to New York and around the world. Through our efforts to conserve these natural habitats, we also aim to promote the study and practice of Zoology.

We oversee five wildlife parks/aquariums in New York City including: The Bronx Zoo, Central Park Zoo, New York Aquarium, Prospect Park Zoo, and The Queens Zoo.

WCS Projects

The Wildlife Conservation Society has continued to make global strides towards long-term conservation across The Americas, Africa, Asia, and Oceania. Over the past 100 years WCS has esablished deep partnerships with communities across 14 different regions, aiming to assist in the protection and preservation of all types of animals and wildlife.
